Batu

is a city located in East Java Province of Indonesia. It is
situated about 20 km to the northwest of Malang. Formerly,
it was a part of Malang Regency; but in 2001, Batu became
an independent city.
A population of 190,000 people, it lies on the southern
slopes of Gunung Welirang. Its population largely consists
of Javanese.
